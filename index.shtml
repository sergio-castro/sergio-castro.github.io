Short bio

Sergio Castro is a PhD candidate and teaching assistant at the Université catholique de Louvain (UCL). His workgroup is the REsearch Laboratory on software Evolution And Software Development technology (RELEASeD), under the direction of professor Kim Mens.
He holds a degree in Computer Engineering specialized in Information Systems from the Escuela Superior Politécnica del Litoral ( ESPOL), Ecuador, and an International Master in Computer Science from the Vrije Universiteit Brussel (VUB), Belgium.
His current research interests are related to multi-paradigm programming, linguistic symbiosis, domain specific languages and program transformation techniques. Other interests involve software evolution and logic meta-programming techniques in general, and co-evolution of design rules and source code in particular.
His previous professional experience has mainly been related to an active participation in the analysis, design and implementation of software systems for leading ecuadorian telecom companies.

Research

Current Research

I am currently working on portable libraries and frameworks for simplifying the interaction between a logic language (Prolog) and an object-oriented language (Java). One of my main objectives is to facilitate, by means of linguistic symbiosis techniques, the creation and evolution of systems composed of both logic and object-oriented modules.

In addition, I am attempting to provide a set of reusable open-source hybrid software components (e.g., GUI components) that may help to create more efficiently such Java-Prolog systems, and that are not coupled to a particular backend Prolog engine.

You can check out my projects in the public domain, on this and other topics, at GitHub
